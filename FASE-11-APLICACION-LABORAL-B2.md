## 🎤 SEMANA 3-4: INTERVIEW PREPARATION

### SCRIPT DE ENTREVISTA TÉCNICA

```
ENTREVISTA: Senior DevOps Engineer (1 hora)

---FORMATO TÍPICO---

Part 1: Tell me about yourself (5 min)
"Hi, thanks for having me. I'm [Name], Senior DevOps Engineer 
with 5 years of experience building and managing cloud 
infrastructure. Most recently at [Company], I led a team of 4 
engineers and reduced our infrastructure costs by 40%.

Before that, I spent 3 years as a DevOps engineer at [Previous], 
where I architected our migration from traditional servers to 
Kubernetes, serving 10M+ daily active users.

I'm passionate about reliability, automation, and building systems 
that scale efficiently. In my free time, I contribute to open 
source projects like [Project] and speak at conferences about 
[Topic].

I'm excited about [Company] because [reason specific to company]."

DURACIÓN: Exactamente 1-2 minutos (practica esto)

---

Part 2: Walk me through a challenging project

"At [Company], we had a critical infrastructure challenge. Our 
single-region deployment was a single point of failure. If one 
region went down, the entire service was unavailable.

The challenge: We served users across 5 continents and needed 
true high availability while minimizing costs.

What I did:
1. Designed multi-region Kubernetes architecture
2. Implemented automated failover using Consul service mesh
3. Set up cross-region replication for stateful services

The result: 99.99% availability (up from 99.9%), reduced 
single-region failure impact from full outage to 30 minutes.

The lesson: Always think about failure modes before they happen."

STRUCTURE:
- SITUATION: Context (2-3 sentences)
- TASK: Challenge you faced (1-2 sentences)
- ACTION: Specific steps you took (3-4 sentences)
- RESULT: Measurable outcome (1-2 sentences)

---

Part 3: Technical questions

Q: "How would you optimize our Kubernetes cluster costs?"
A: "I'd start with three areas:

First, right-sizing. Most teams over-provision by 40-50%. I'd use 
Prometheus metrics to identify actual resource usage.

Second, reserved instances. On AWS, using reserved instances for 
base load provides 30-40% savings compared to on-demand.

Third, autoscaling. Horizontal Pod Autoscaler (HPA) based on 
custom metrics ensures we're not paying for unused capacity.

Together, these typically reduce costs by 40-50%. In my previous 
role, we reduced costs from $8,500/month to $5,100/month."

STRUCTURE:
- Acknowledge the question
- Show you understand the problem
- Provide 2-3 concrete solutions
- Back up with numbers/experience
- Ask clarifying question if needed

---

Part 4: Do you have questions for us?

ASK:
✓ "What does success look like for this role in the first 90 days?"
✓ "How do you measure engineering productivity?"
✓ "What's the biggest technical challenge your team is facing?"
✓ "How do you handle on-call and incident response?"

DON'T ASK:
✗ "How much vacation?" (ask at offer stage)
✗ "Remote work flexibility?" (probably stated in job posting)
✗ "When do I get promoted?" (too early)
✗ Generic questions you could Google

---

VOCABULARIO:
✓ "Tell me about a time when..." = Cuéntame de un momento cuando...
✓ "Walk me through" = Guíame a través de
✓ "Challenge" = Desafío
✓ "Trade-off" = Compensación
✓ "Scalability" = Escalabilidad
✓ "High availability" = Alta disponibilidad
✓ "Failover" = Conmutación por error
✓ "Deployment" = Despliegue
✓ "In production" = En producción
✓ "Root cause" = Causa raíz
```

## 💰 SEMANA 5: SALARY NEGOTIATION

### SCRIPT DE NEGOCIACIÓN SALARIAL

```
CONTEXTO:
Oferta inicial: $8,000/mes
Esperado: $12,000/mes

AFTER OFFER:
Email: "Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the opportunity. 
Before I accept, I'd like to discuss the compensation.

Based on my research and experience:
- Senior DevOps Engineer in US market: $12K-16K/month
- My background: 5+ years, 40% cost reductions, team leadership
- Market rate for remote DevOps: $10K-14K/month

I'd like to request $12,000/month base salary. Additionally, I'd 
appreciate clarity on:
- Equity/stock options
- Bonus structure
- Health insurance (for remote)
- Professional development budget

I'm very interested in joining your team. Let's find a number that 
works for both of us."

---

"I understand budget constraints. What if we structured it as:
- Base: $10,000/month
- Sign-on bonus: $5,000 (one-time)

This gives me $10K/month recurring and you maintain budget 
initially."

---

"I appreciate being direct. Let me ask: what conditions would 
allow a salary review in 3 months?
- Hit specific targets on cost reduction
- Team/scope expansion
- Additional certifications or skills"

## 🌐 SEMANA 6: REMOTE WORK COMMUNICATION

### HERRAMIENTAS Y COMUNICACIÓN REMOTA

```
TOOLS YOU'LL USE:

1. SLACK
   - Professional tone even in casual channels
   - Example: "Hey team, I've completed the Kubernetes upgrade. 
     Documentation: [link]. Rollout plan: [link]"
   - Avoid: Memes, casual language, unclear messages
   - Response time: < 2 hours for important messages

2. ASYNC DOCUMENTATION
   - Clear setup instructions
   - Screenshots with explanations
   - Video walkthrough for complex processes
   - Update docs before asking "how do I...?"

3. VIDEO CALLS (Zoom/Meet)
   - Professional background or blurred
   - Camera on (build trust)
   - Mute when not speaking
   - Clear audio (headphone + mic)
   - Dress professionally (shirts at least)

4. GITHUB/VERSION CONTROL
   - Clear PR descriptions
   - Link to tickets/issues
   - Explain changes in English
   - Code comments in English

5. EMAIL
   - Professional tone
   - Clear subject line
   - Specific asks
   - Deadline if needed

---

EJEMPLOS DE COMUNICACIÓN REMOTA:

SLACK MESSAGE (GOOD):
"@devops-team
The Kubernetes cluster upgrade completed successfully. No issues. 
All services running normally.

- Rollback plan tested: ✅
- Documentation: [link]
- Next steps: [link]"
